About us

More than just science.

The Fallon Science Olympiad is more than just learning about science. It encourages students to work as a team toward a shared goal, with every subgroup contributing their part. It is also like running a marathon, a test of persistence and dedication to participate in 23 events in a single day as a team effort.

The topics of study and participation can range from earth sciences to genetics, aeronautical engineering, and even deciphering coding! In addition to that, there are hands-on building and lab events to engage students and help them learn about the different fields of STEM, while picking up vital skills such as critical thinking, teamwork, and so much more.

For more information, check out the official Science Olympiad Website.

How it works

The basics.

Stuff students & parents should know before signing up.

🧪

Tryouts

Every student is tested before being accepted. Only 15 make the team. We use the tryouts to build balanced event groups so everyone's set up for success.

💸

Parent-funded

The program is parent-funded. It takes qualifying students from invitationals and regionals all the way up to nationals.

🚗

Getting around

Parents handle driving students to and from competitions. We coordinate the rest.

🚀

Day to day

What you'll actually do.

🎯

Compete in 23 events

Anatomy & Physiology, Codebusters, and a lot more. Every event contributes to the team score.

⚙️

Build & test

Engineering events let you design, build, and re-test things until they work.

📖

Study & specialize

For knowledge events you'll go deep on a topic and basically become the team's go-to person on it.

🔓

Crack codes

Cipher events test how fast you can spot patterns and decode under pressure. Surprisingly fun.

🤝

Work in pairs

Most events are run in pairs or trios. You'll get really good at trusting your partner.

✈️

Travel to comps

Invitationals, Regionals, States, Nationals. Wear the Fallon shirt with pride.

🔬
SciOly has a way of turning a casual interest into a real passion. When you go deep on a subject — actually studying it, competing with it, and seeing it pay off on comp day — you start to find out what genuinely excites you. That kind of clarity about what you love in STEM is something most people spend years trying to figure out.
